# Oxygen Requirements

[91.211]()

## Non-Pressurized Cabin

### Above 12,500 MSL cabin pressure altitude

- required flight crew must use supplemental oxygen while above this altitude for more than 30 minutes
- this is per flight, you must land to reset the 30 minutes

### Above 14,000 MSL cabin pressure altitude

- required flight crew must use supplemental oxygen

### Above 15,000 MSL cabin pressure altitude

- all occupants must be provided supplemental oxygen

## Pressurized Cabin

### Above FL 250

- all occupants must have a 10 minute supply of supplemental oxygen available
- this is for use in the event of a loss of cabin pressurization

### Above FL 350

#### Single Pilot

- pilot must wear and use an oxygen mask
- mask must either always supply oxygen, or automatically supply oxygen whenever the cabin pressure altitude exceeds 14,000 MSL

#### Two Pilots below FL 410

- pilots are not required to wear an oxygen mask, as long as they each have a quick-donning oxygen mask available
- must be able to be placed on the face with one hand in 5 seconds
- if a pilot must leave the controls, the other must use an oxygen mask
